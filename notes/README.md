# Aktiv vs Passiv
## Aktiv
* Doer of the action is always there and it is Nominative

Example: Rahul will deliver the packet.

## Passiv
It is used when:
1. Doer of action is absent  or
2. Doer is present with a preposition (by)

Example : **Doer of action is Absent** -> The packer will be delivered tomorrow

Example of **Rule 2** -> The packet will be delivered tomorrow by Rahul/Employee

* Like Aktiv sentence, we also have tenses in Passiv, which are given below:

### Präsens

### Präteritum

### Partizip Perfekt

### Future

### Modal Verb

## Summary of Aktiv vs Passiv

Sentence is Aktiv when: `Doer = Nominativ`

Sentence is Passiv when: 
1. Does is not there or 
2. Doer is at the end with a preposition "by"

# Zu + Infintiv


## Ausnahmen für Zu + Infintiv

## Additional Cases für Zu + Infintiv

1. um (in order to) --- zu + inf
2. statt (instead of) --- zu + inf
3. ohne (without) --- zu + inf

1. I take a walk in order to be relived --> Ich mache einen Spaziergang, um erleichtert zu sein

2. I came to Germany, instead of traveling to Australia --> Ich bin nach Deutschland gekommen, statt nach Australia zu reisen

3. You can't go without meeting me --> Du kannst nicht gehen, ohne mich zu treffen

## Structure Discussion About Zu + Infintiv

### Erste Ausnahme

**This rule applies when:**

1. Second verb is present with **by** (by + 2nd Verb -> `indem` als Konnektor)
Example: I learn German by reading novels 

**How would be sentence structure?**
* Always new sentence starts with "indem" Konnektor (Nominativ & Verb am ende)
* Tense of both the sentences should be same

### Zweite Ausnahme
**This rule applies when:**
1. Second verb is present with **while** 
	```
	1. (while + 2nd Verb -> `beim` + verb als Nomen)
	2. (while + 2nd Verb -> `beim` + verb (informal form) after capital
```
Example: I don't speak while eating.

## wechsel Präposition vs Keine Präposition

wechsel präposition --> Adds an object
Keine präposition  --> No object
 	```
	wechsel         vs    Keine

	unter				unten
	hinter				hinten
	vor					vorne
	über				oben
	```

Examples:

1. Ich gehen unten (I am going downstairs)
2. Ich gehe oben (I am going up/upstairs)
3. Er steht vorne (he is standing infront)
4. Er steht vor mir. (wechsel) (he is standing infront of me)

## How to use two Helping Verbs in a Sentence?

Rule:

```Whenever we have 2 helping verbs in a sentence, the second helping verb goes to the extreme end of the sentence in the infinitive form```

Example: 
	1. I will not be able to do it.   (will = werden (h.v), able to = Können, do = machen)
	
	Ich werde es nicht machen können
